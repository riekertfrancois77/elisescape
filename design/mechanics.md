# Mechanics

> What the player actually *does*, moment to moment. Drafted after the game
> direction is chosen (the decision, review, and harness steps of Module 1).
> Detailed design happens in later modules — this file starts as the
> high-level sketch.

## Core Loop

**Enter a room → look closely → notice what's "off" → solve it → a secret
opens → cross a suspect off the list → move deeper.**

Curiosity pulls the player into a room; careful observation reveals the one
detail that's wrong (the painting-and-statue moment); fixing/solving it opens
the way forward *and* teaches something about a party guest. Each solve makes
the player feel clever and reignites curiosity for the next room. The loop
stays fun because every room is a fresh, fair "aha," and every aha narrows the
list of who the killer could be.

## Player Verbs

- **Look / Examine** — inspect objects, rooms, and details closely (the heart
  of the game).
- **Notice / Compare** — spot what's out of place; compare things that should
  match (statue vs. painting) and catch the one that's off.
- **Collect clues (evidence)** — pick up and remember clues; the killer leaves
  some on purpose.
- **Deduce** — connect clues to eliminate suspects and narrow down which guest
  is the culprit (Sherlock-style).
- **Solve / Fix** — act on a noticed detail to open a secret passage to the
  next room.
- **Remember** — carry an early clue forward; it becomes the key near the end.

## Challenge & Progression

- **The pushback:** rooms get trickier; details get more hidden; some clues are
  **red herrings** (innocent guests who *look* suspicious).
- **The staircase (Maya's note):** each room teaches a noticing skill the player
  *reuses* later — the game trains the player to think like a detective.
- **The nearly-quit wall:** a late dead-end that seems to give nothing — until
  the player remembers the clue from the very first room (setup & payoff).
- **The finale:** eliminate enough suspects and connect the trail to name the
  culprit hiding among the party guests, and reveal where they've been hiding.

# Next Room — The Library (Blueprint)

> **Designed by Eli (Creative Director & Game Designer), 2026-07-07 — Module 8.**
> This is *Eli's* design, in his head first. Claude only pressure-tested it.
> It is the **blueprint to build in Module 10** — do not build it before then.
> The room after the Grand Foyer, reached through the oak door the detective
> unlocks at the end of Version 0.1.

## The Room

A **library**. Books and shelves everywhere — floor-to-ceiling, packed, a room
made of hiding places. In the middle stands **one table, lit by candlelight**.
The single warm table in a cluttered room gives the player's eye a focal point
(a deliberate design choice by Eli — guide the eye).

Mood carries over from Module 7's art direction: **warm but wrong, grand and
rich, a touch of creepy** — gold, mahogany, deep crimson, with cold shadow at
the edges. A cozy room full of secrets (Pillar 4: *A Cozy House That Hides
Everything*).

## What's "Off" (the centerpiece puzzle)

A **statue** stands in the library that **matches the host's portrait from the
foyer** — except it's **slightly wrong**: the statue is **holding the wrong
object.** In the portrait the host is painted mid-toast, a **goblet of deep red
wine raised high in his right hand**. The statue's hand holds something else.

The detective has to **notice the mismatch** and **fix what the statue is
holding** — give it the raised **goblet of wine** to match the portrait.

## The Clue (why it's FAIR — Eli's Rule 1)

- The correct answer lives in the **foyer portrait**, which now (as of Version
  0.5) explicitly shows the host **holding a goblet of wine, raised in a toast**.
  The setup is planted early, on purpose.
- The player **can travel back between rooms**, so the portrait is always a
  **findable, re-checkable clue** — no relying on perfect memory, no guessing.
  *(Eli defended this himself under pressure-testing.)*

## The Deduction (why it's HARD & CLEVER — Eli's Rule 2)

- It rewards the player who **paid attention in room one**. The clever detective
  remembers "that statue looks like the portrait..." and goes to check.
- It's **setup and payoff**: something seen early (the portrait) comes back and
  matters later. The same promise the win screen already makes about the clock.
- The action — **adjust the wrong thing to match** — mirrors *setting the clock
  hands* in the foyer, so the game feels consistent.

## The Payoff

Fix the statue (give it the goblet of wine) and a **hidden room slides open** —
a secret passage revealed behind or beneath the statue. It **rewards** the solve
*and* **pulls the player deeper** into the manor. (What's inside the hidden room
is future design.)

## Room Depth (Eli's note)

The statue is the room's **centerpiece, not its only puzzle** — Eli's call. The
library is designed to hold **more** puzzles/clues among the shelves (a book out
of place, the candle, a gap on a shelf…). Those are open for later design; the
statue puzzle is the locked one.

## Open Items for the Module 10 Build

- **Room navigation:** the game needs a way to travel **between the foyer and
  the library** (so the portrait stays checkable). Not built yet.
- **The statue's adjustable object:** the wrong object it starts with, and how
  the player swaps it to the goblet of wine (click to cycle? drag?).
- **The hidden room:** what it looks like and what's inside (future design).
- Keep both of **Eli's Puzzle Rules**: FAIR (clues findable) and HARD & CLEVER
  (earned "aha," no hand-holding).

## Eli's Puzzle Rules (his design checklist, from Module 8)

1. **FAIR** — every answer comes from clues the player can find. No guessing.
2. **HARD & CLEVER** — the player has to *work* to connect the clues; they earn
   the "aha," never get handed it.

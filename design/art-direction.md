# Art Direction

> The look and mood of the game. Sketched at the end of Module 1; deep work
> happens in Module 10 (AI Art, Audio and Atmosphere).

## Visual Mood

**Warm · Elegant · Quietly Unsettling.** Eli's ruling (Studio Review,
2026-07-06): *somewhere in between* cozy-storybook and dark-and-tense. The
mansion is inviting and beautiful on the surface, with real shadows and
tension underneath. Not gory, not babyish — gripping.

**Why in-between wins:** the warmth *lowers the player's guard*, so when a
detail is wrong (a cold draft, a portrait's eyes, a guest standing too still),
it hits far harder. Contrast is what makes "Notice What's Off" pop. If
everything were spooky, nothing would stand out.

## Color & Light

Warm base — glowing lamplight, firelight, rich wood, golds and deep reds of an
early-1900s party. Cool, shadowy accents seep in at the edges: blue-grey
darkness in corners, doorways, and anywhere a secret hides. Light guides the
eye toward what matters; shadow hides what the player must *notice*.

## Style References

- **Sherlock Holmes** (Eli's north star) — the *observing* mindset; a refined,
  period detective mood.
- Early-1900s grand-mansion setting; a glittering house party as the surface,
  secrets and passages underneath.

## Sound & Atmosphere (early notes)

Distant party music and murmured conversation as a warm backdrop — then it
thins out into creaks, a ticking clock, and silence in the rooms where the
player is alone with the clues. Sound should make cozy feel cozy, so the quiet
moments feel tense. *(Deep work in Module 10.)*

## The Game in One Sentence

*A famous detective is locked inside a cozy 1900s mansion for one night with a
killer who leaves clues on purpose — and must notice what's "off," room by
room, to corner them before dawn.*

**Working title:** *One Night at Thornwood Manor* (title not final — Eli's call).

## Why This Game Exists

To make the player *feel like Sherlock Holmes* — curious while exploring, and
clever when they notice the tiny detail everyone else missed. It rewards
**observation** over reflexes, and **deduction** over luck.

## What Players Should Say After Playing

*"I felt like a real detective — everything I needed was right there, and I
was the one who saw it."*

## The Promise

- **Always:** fair clues hidden in plain sight; every solution earnable by
  looking closer and thinking clearer; a warm world that hides its secrets.
- **Never:** cheap tricks, random guesses, or answers that come from luck
  instead of noticing.

## The Chosen Direction (First Big Decision, 2026-07-06)

**Direction A — "One Night at Thornwood Manor."** A single tense evening. The
detective arrives just after the murder; the killer is still hidden inside, so
no one leaves until it's solved. The player moves **room by room, deeper into
the mansion**, each room a self-contained "notice what's off" puzzle whose
solution opens a secret passage to the next — following the killer's trail
inward. A clue from the very first room becomes the key to the final
confrontation, cornering the culprit in their hiding place.

*Directions B ("The Detective's Notebook" — open, connect-the-clues) and C
("The Clue-Leaver's Game" — move-for-move duel) were not chosen, but their
flavors may be borrowed inside A: B's Sherlock-style deductions and C's
taunting, present villain.*

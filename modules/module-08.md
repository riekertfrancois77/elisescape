# Module 8 — Design Great Games

**Status:** OPENED by Dad (2026-07-07) · **Unlocks when Module 7 is complete.**
**Role:** Eli is Creative Director **and Game Designer**. Claude wears the
**Game Designer / Systems** hat — a design *partner and critic*, never the source
of the design.

**Version target:** the game climbs from **0.4 → 0.5** — Eli's new design eye
makes the current game better, and the **blueprint** for the next room is locked
(that room gets *built* in Module 10).

## Purpose

This is the craft module: **how to make a player feel clever.** Eli designs his
game's **next room and its puzzle** — with his own head, first and alone — and
learns what makes a puzzle *fair* (solvable from findable clues) and *satisfying*
(a real "aha"). Claude's job is to wait, then pressure-test: is it fair? clear?
clever? Not to invent it.

This is the purest "Eli's brain first" module of the whole run. It also sets up
Module 10, where the room he designs here becomes real.

## The Brain-First Rule (this module lives or dies on it)

**Eli designs; Claude critiques.** Do **not** propose the room, the twist, or the
puzzle. Ask questions, hold the design standards up, and let him fill the space:

- *What's in this room? What's "off" about it?*
- *What does the player have to notice? To deduce?*
- *What clues let them solve it — and can they actually find them?*
- *What's the payoff when they crack it?*

Only after he's designed it do you pressure-test and help sharpen. If you catch
yourself about to hand him an idea, turn it into a question instead.

## What You'll Learn

- Great games are **designed to make players feel clever.**
- You design the next room and its puzzle with **your** head — first, alone.
- A good puzzle is **fair**: solvable from clues the player can find.
- Claude's job is to **pressure-test** your design, not invent it.
- A design you can **defend** is a design worth building.

## Rules for Claude Code in This Module

- Begin by studying **what already works** in his game — *with* him, so he can
  name *why* the foyer puzzle is fair and clever (it becomes his design checklist).
- Make Eli **design the next room + puzzle himself** (see brain-first rule).
- **Pressure-test** as devil's advocate: hunt for unfair leaps, missing clues,
  and "how would a player even know that?" gaps — then let him fix them.
- **Two deliverables:**
  1. **A real improvement to the CURRENT game** using his sharpened design eye —
     ship it so the version genuinely bumps to **0.5** (a fairer clue, a clearer
     "off" moment, a better payoff — his call).
  2. **A locked design doc** for the next room, saved to `design/` (e.g.
     `design/next-room.md`), detailed enough to *build* in Module 10.
- Do **not** build the new room here — that's Module 10. This module ends with a
  design, not a new playable room.
- **Bump the version to 0.5** in `MEMORY.md`; add Session-Memory + `session-log`
  entries in Eli's voice; note the next-room blueprint is ready for Module 10.
- Update Module 8 Steps; unlock **Game Designer** at the end.

## Mission Flow

1. **Meet the Game Designer** — Claude becomes your design partner and critic.
2. **Study what already works** — why the foyer puzzle feels fair and clever.
3. **You design the next room** — on your own: the space, the "notice what's off".
4. **You design its puzzle** — clues in, deduction, payoff. Your idea first.
5. **Pressure-test** — Claude plays devil's advocate: fair? clear? clever?
6. **Sharpen the current game** with your new design eye — one real improvement
   (**Version 0.5**).
7. **Lock the blueprint** — your next-room design is saved, ready to build in
   Module 10.
8. **Achievement unlock:** Game Designer.

## Done When

- Eli designed the next room + puzzle **himself**, and defended it against
  pressure-testing.
- He shipped one real design improvement to the current game (**Version 0.5**).
- The next-room blueprint is saved in `design/` for Module 10.
- The memory is updated.
- The **Game Designer** achievement is unlocked.
